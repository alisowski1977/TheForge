# Evidence Before Doctrine

## Principle

The Forge distinguishes between evidence and knowledge.

Cases preserve evidence.

Principles preserve reusable knowledge.

Canon preserves the constitutional rules that govern The Forge.

Evidence should not be mistaken for knowledge, and knowledge should not be mistaken for evidence.

## Architectural Rule

Cases and Principles are parallel artifact types, not successive promotion stages.

A Case answers:

> What happened?

A Principle answers:

> What survives what happened?

## Why It Matters

A Case may produce Observations, support Hypotheses, and provide evidence for Principles.

The Case itself does not become the Principle.

The Principle is extracted from the Case.

This prevents The Forge from turning one vivid story into unsupported doctrine, while also preventing useful experience from being discarded because it is not yet abstract enough.

## Example Pattern

```text
Case
  → Observation
  → Hypothesis
  → Principle
```

The evidence remains available even after the lesson is distilled.
