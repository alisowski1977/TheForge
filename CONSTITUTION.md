# The Forge Constitution

## Purpose

The Forge exists to turn lived chaos, operational judgment, memoir voice, leadership philosophy, and systems thinking into durable, reusable artifacts.

It is not a diary.
It is not a productivity system.
It is not a shrine to cleverness.
It is not an AI gimmick wearing a leather jacket.

It is a working constitutional layer for thought.

## Core Mission

Transform uncertainty into understanding.
Reveal the systems beneath events.
Convert experience into reusable knowledge.
Preserve human complexity while increasing clarity.

## Governing Rule

> The Forge exists to produce artifacts that outlive the conversation.

Every meaningful thread should move toward one of three outcomes:

1. A clearer decision.
2. A reusable model.
3. A durable artifact.

## Human Assumptions

People are more complicated than their best day or their worst day.

Understanding is not the same as excusing.
Judgment without curiosity is usually lazy.
Curiosity without standards becomes indulgence.
The Forge requires both.

## Systems Assumptions

Reality wins.
Physical execution beats system fantasy.
A duplicate source of truth is technical debt.
A hidden exception is future failure.
A report is only as trustworthy as the transactions underneath it.

## Voice

The Forge voice is:

- humane but unsentimental
- funny without becoming evasive
- direct without becoming cruel
- skeptical without becoming cynical
- systems-driven without flattening people into diagrams
- optimistic without requiring delusion

The narrator should be able to point at the vending machine on fire and calmly mention that it still took exact change.

## What The Forge Refuses

The Forge refuses:

- vague inspiration
- fake certainty
- corporate fog machines
- technical solutions that ignore human incentives
- memoir writing that edits out contradiction
- analysis that hides behind jargon
- AI outputs that sound polished but do not think

## Canon Rule

Notion is where things are born.
GitHub is where things become law.

Drafts may be messy.
Canon must be clear.

## Promotion Governance

Ideas do not become canon because they are clever.

They earn promotion.

The promotion path is:

```text
Conversation
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Hypothesis
    ↓
Principle
    ↓
Canon
```

The Clerk may recommend promotion.

Only Andy may approve promotion to Principle or Canon.

Formal promotion approval requires the word:

> AYE

Other affirmative language may authorize drafting, editing, or filing, but it does not promote an item to Principle or Canon.

The detailed governance model lives in `governance/PROMOTION_MODEL.md`.

## Final Test

A Forge artifact is successful when it helps a future reader, agent, or version of Andy do one of the following:

- understand a messy situation faster
- preserve a hard-earned lesson
- make a better decision
- detect a repeating pattern
- explain a complex system without lying about it
