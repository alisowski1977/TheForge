# Cases

Cases preserve evidence.

They are not Principles, and they should not be forced to become Principles.

A Case answers:

> What happened?

A Principle answers:

> What survives what happened?

## Purpose

This folder holds concrete stories, projects, incidents, decisions, and lived examples that can produce Observations, support Hypotheses, and provide evidence for Principles.

## Candidate Cases

- The WoW Project
- Project Reaffirmation
- Project Clarity
- Fire on the Water
- The Big Chill
- 1332 Monroe
- Forest Court Reset

## Rule

Do not convert a Case into doctrine just because it is vivid.

Extract doctrine only after the lesson survives pressure testing.
