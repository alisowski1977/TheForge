# Promotion Model

## Purpose

The Forge does not treat every good sentence, useful idea, or sharp observation as canon.

Ideas earn promotion.

The promotion model protects The Forge from becoming a junk drawer of clever fragments while still allowing raw thinking to survive long enough to become useful.

## Promotion Ladder

```text
Conversation
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Hypothesis
    ↓
Principle
    ↓
Canon
```

## Stages

### 1. Conversation

Raw material.

A conversation may contain jokes, doubts, contradictions, fragments, arguments, memories, technical notes, or half-formed ideas.

Conversation is not doctrine.

### 2. Observation

A noticed pattern.

An observation says:

> Something happened often enough, clearly enough, or strangely enough that it should not be lost.

An observation does not yet claim to be generally true.

### 3. Hypothesis

A proposed explanation.

A hypothesis says:

> I think this pattern means something.

A hypothesis should survive critique before promotion.

### 4. Principle

A reusable model.

A principle says:

> This has been tested enough to guide future reasoning.

Principles may apply to memoir, ERP architecture, leadership, governance, relationships, operating systems, or human behavior.

### 5. Canon

Constitutional knowledge.

Canon says:

> This is part of The Forge until deliberately amended.

Canon should be rare, clear, durable, and explicitly approved.

## Governance Rule

The Clerk may recommend promotion.

Only Andy may approve promotion to Principle or Canon.

Formal approval requires the word:

> AYE

Other affirmative language may authorize ordinary drafting, editing, or filing, but it does not promote an item to Principle or Canon.

## No Automatic Promotion

Nothing becomes canon merely because it was well-written, emotionally satisfying, funny, or useful in the moment.

A strong artifact may still remain a draft.

A clever idea may still remain an observation.

A principle may still fail to earn canon.

## Review Questions

Before promoting an item, ask:

- Is it true enough to preserve?
- Is it useful beyond the original conversation?
- Has it survived challenge?
- Is it clearly worded?
- Does it belong in Notion, GitHub, or both?
- Is it an observation, hypothesis, principle, or canon?

## Closing Ceremony

When a session is closed, The Forge should review what emerged and classify it:

- remain conversation
- record as observation
- draft as hypothesis
- promote to principle
- propose for canon

The closing review should not promote anything to Principle or Canon without AYE.

## Short Rule

Ideas do not become canon because they are clever.

They become canon because they earned it.
